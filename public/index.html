FAQ

Common questions

What is an .eml file?

A standard email message format used by Outlook, Thunderbird, Apple Mail, and other email clients. It stores the message text, sender/recipient details, and any attachments in one file.

Does this upload my emails anywhere?

No. Parsing and PDF rendering happen locally in your browser. Only account sign-in and the one-time purchase check talk to a server — neither ever sees your email content.

Can I convert multiple files at once?

Yes — batch conversion is supported, up to 20 files at a time.

Will attachments and images be included?

Yes. Embedded images, formatting, and attachments are preserved in the final PDF.

What email clients export .eml files?

Outlook, Thunderbird, Apple Mail, and most webmail export tools (including Gmail via "Download message") all produce standard .eml files compatible with this extension.

Is the Lifetime unlock a subscription?

No — it's a single one-time payment tied to your Google account. There's no recurring billing, and access carries over if you reinstall or switch devices.
